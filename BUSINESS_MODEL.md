# TimorMart - Business Model

## Executive Summary

**TimorMart** is a C2C (Consumer-to-Consumer) marketplace platform connecting buyers and sellers across Timor-Leste. The platform addresses the fragmented retail market in Timor-Leste by providing a centralized, secure digital marketplace for buying and selling new and second-hand products locally.

---

## 1. Value Propositions

### For Sellers
- **Expanded Market Reach**: Access buyers across all municipalities in Timor-Leste without expensive physical stores
- **Low Startup Costs**: No storefront rental or inventory management requirements
- **Easy Product Management**: Intuitive dashboard to list, edit, and track products
- **Buyer Trust**: Platform-managed reviews and seller verification increase credibility
- **Localized Experience**: Multi-language support (English & Tetum) and local payment options

### For Buyers
- **Convenient Shopping**: Browse products from home with delivery options
- **Product Variety**: Access new and second-hand items across multiple categories
- **Transparent Pricing**: Clear pricing with condition indicators (new vs. second-hand)
- **Community Trust**: Verified sellers with comment ratings and sentiment analysis
- **Local Support**: Contact sellers directly via WhatsApp integration
- **Multi-language Interface**: Browse in English or Tetum

### For the Platform
- **Market Digitization**: Pioneer digital commerce in Timor-Leste's emerging market
- **Economic Growth**: Reduce informal economy, increase tax base, create jobs
- **Data Insights**: Understand consumer preferences and market trends

---

## 2. Revenue Streams

### Primary (Active)
1. **Commission on Sales** (15-20% of transaction value)
   - Applied when seller's product is sold
   - Higher rate for featured listings (30%)
   - Tiered discounts for high-volume sellers (100+ products)

2. **Featured Product Listing** ($2-5 per month)
   - Increased visibility on homepage
   - Placement in search results
   - Priority in category pages

### Secondary (Future)
3. **Seller Subscription Tiers**
   - **Basic**: Free (limited listings)
   - **Pro**: $5/month (unlimited listings, analytics)
   - **Enterprise**: $20/month (API access, bulk tools, dedicated support)

4. **Advertising**
   - Category takeovers ($100/month)
   - Sponsored product slots ($2 per click)
   - Brand pages for manufacturers

5. **Services**
   - Product photography service
   - Professional seller setup ($50 one-time)
   - Shipping label integration ($0.50 per label)

6. **Payment Processing Fee**
   - Merchant discount rate on payment gateway
   - Estimated 2-3% of transaction value

## 3. Customer Segments

### Primary Segments
1. **Individual Sellers** (Informal Traders)
   - Small business owners selling 5-50 products
   - Market traders moving online
   - Target: 2,000+ sellers in Year 1

2. **Individual Buyers** (Local Consumers)
   - Ages 18-55, digitally literate
   - Seeking convenience and product variety
   - Target: 10,000+ registered buyers in Year 1

3. **Micro-Entrepreneurs** (Growth Segment)
   - Small traders looking to scale
   - Potentially upgrade to Pro tier

### Secondary Segments
4. **SMEs & Wholesalers** (Future)
   - Bulk product sellers
   - Dropshippers
   - Regional distributors

5. **Government/NGOs** (Impact)
   - Public procurement marketplace
   - Artisan/women's cooperative support

## 8. Go-To-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Soft Launch (Months 1-3)
- **Target**: Initial user base in Dili and surrounding areas
- **Tactics**: 
  - Partner with local business associations
  - Influencer early adopters (10-20 sellers)
  - Focus groups with target buyers
  - WhatsApp group marketing
- **Goals**: 100-200 sellers, 1,000-2,000 buyers

### Phase 2: Expansion (Months 4-12)
- **Target**: Nationwide coverage across all municipalities
- **Tactics**:
  - Regional marketing campaigns
  - Partner with transport companies for delivery
  - Radio/TV advertising in Tetum
  - SME training workshops
- **Goals**: 500-1,000 sellers, 5,000-10,000 buyers

### Phase 3: Scaling (Year 2+)
- **Target**: Monetization and profitability
- **Tactics**:
  - Subscription tier rollout
  - Advertising marketplace
  - Strategic partnerships with logistics/payments
  - Expansion to B2B (wholesale) segment

## 12. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

### Key Risks
| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|-----------|
| Low internet penetration | Revenue ceiling | Medium | Partner with telcos for mobile-first app |
| Payment adoption | User friction | Medium | Multiple payment methods (cash, mobile money) |
| Fraud & disputes | Trust erosion | Medium | Seller verification, escrow system, dispute resolution |
| Competition from Facebook | User acquisition | High | Focus on local trust, service quality |
| Regulatory uncertainty | Operations | Low | Engage with government, compliance-first approach |
| Logistics barriers | Order fulfillment | High | Partner with existing transport networks |

### Mitigation Strategies
- ✅ Build trust through strict moderation and seller verification
- ✅ Offer multiple payment options (cash on delivery, mobile money, bank transfer)
- ✅ Implement dispute resolution and buyer protection
- ✅ Focus on local customer support in Tetum
- ✅ Partner with established logistics providers
- ✅ Engage proactively with government/regulators

## 13. Success Metrics (KPIs)

### User Metrics
- **Monthly Active Users (MAU)**: Sellers & Buyers
- **User Retention**: Month-over-month cohort retention
- **New User Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: $ per user
- **Lifetime Value (LTV)**: Total revenue per user

### Marketplace Metrics
- **Gross Merchandise Value (GMV)**: Total transaction value
- **Average Order Value (AOV)**: Revenue per transaction
- **Take Rate**: Revenue ÷ GMV
- **Conversion Rate**: Browsers to buyers

### Business Metrics
- **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)**: Subscription tier revenue
- **Gross Profit Margin**: (Revenue - COGS) ÷ Revenue
- **Customer Acquisition Cost Payback**: Months to recoup CAC
- **Break-Even Point**: Monthly GMV/transactions needed
